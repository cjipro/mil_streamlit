6. Failure Mode Definitions

To be added to MIL_SCHEMA.yaml as a failure_modes block. Three operational states. Each has a defined response — not just awareness.

SILENCE — No Signals
Trigger: No signals detected for a monitored competitor in a 48-hour window.
Response:
Log SILENCE_FLAG with timestamp and competitor ID
Do not interpret as good news — silence is ambiguous, not positive
Morning Briefing notes: 'No signal detected — possible scraper failure or genuine absence. Check harvester health first.'
Owner checks voice_intelligence_agent.py logs before drawing any conclusion
If harvester healthy and silence genuine — log as data point, not a finding

CONTRADICTION — Signals Conflict
Trigger: Two or more signals from different platforms point in opposite directions (e.g. App Store rating stable, Reddit complaint cluster spiking).
Response:
Log as CONTRADICTED finding — same response mode as CJI Pulse uses
Do not flatten. Do not average. Do not suppress.
Present both signals in Inference Card with source trust weights visible
Human countersign required before any action is taken
CONTRADICTED is information, not a failure — it is the system working correctly

NO CHRONICLE MATCH — Unanchored Signal
Trigger: Inference requested but no CHRONICLE entry matches the signal pattern above similarity threshold.
Response:
Finding logged as UNANCHORED and held — it does not reach Morning Briefing
It does not get trained on under any circumstances
Appears in weekly Unanchored Signals digest for Hussain to review
These are candidates for new CHRONICLE entries, not inferences
Article Zero governs: unverified certainty does not get reported

Nico Zhao's Law extended to failure modes: the system cannot infer its way out of a gap in the CHRONICLE. Honesty about what cannot be known is the only permitted response.
