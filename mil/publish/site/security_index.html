Current production state

What CJI does today.

These capabilities are live in production as of April 2026. Each item is verifiable from the codebase or from direct inspection of the running service.

  • Live UK GDPR-compliant privacy notice with a named data controller, published at /privacy/. No analytics, no tracking pixels, no cookies on the public surface.
  • Live RFC 9116 security disclosure contact at /.well-known/security.txt. Preferred channel: security@cjipro.com.
  • Live Cloudflare-fronted hosting with TLS termination and DDoS mitigation on all public paths. Cloudflare Email Routing handles inbound mail for hello@cjipro.com and security@cjipro.com.
  • Live WorkOS-backed authentication for the alpha briefing surface. Magic-link email-only flow — no passwords stored, no SMS MFA. Explicit anti-SIM-swap design; passkeys preferred via the WorkOS-managed flow.
  • Live Allowlist-based admission gate. Access to the briefing surface requires explicit approval; self-service signup requests are held in a pending queue until an administrator approves each one individually.
  • Live Hash-chained immutable audit log for all authentication events (MIL-65). Each row references the hash of the previous row; tampering with any row invalidates all subsequent rows. PII fields (IP address, user agent, JWT subject) are hashed with daily-rotating salts — not stored in plaintext.
  • Live Per-tenant audit log export (MIL-72). Tenants can request a JSONL or CSV export of their own authentication events via the admin interface. Internal hash columns are excluded from exports.
  • Live Cloudflare Worker edge bouncer enforcing JWT validation on every request to the authenticated briefing surface (MIL-61). Invalid or missing tokens are redirected to the login flow before the origin receives the request.
  • Live WAF rate-limiting rules at the Cloudflare edge covering the signup form, admin API, and the authorisation entry point.
  • Live Zero Entanglement enforcement between MIL (public market signal) and Pulse (private internal telemetry). The boundary is enforced at build time by a dedicated validator (scripts/validate_mil_import_rule.py); a violation breaks the build. The two surfaces never share infrastructure.
  • Live The public sample briefing at /insights/sample-briefing/ uses a fictional firm (Kestrel Cover) and entirely synthetic customer voice. No real customer data appears on the public surface.
Honest accounting

What CJI does not yet hold.

We are in an early alpha stage. The formal certifications below are on the roadmap and are being pursued concurrently with first paid engagements, not as a precondition for them. This section is here because we believe the absence of a certification is more important to disclose than its presence.

  • Roadmap SOC 2 Type 1 — planned alongside first paid engagements. We will not claim SOC 2 compliance until the report is issued by an independent auditor.
  • Roadmap ISO 27001 — information-security management system certification. Planned; not yet initiated. The controls described on these pages are consistent with ISO 27001 intent but have not been independently verified against the standard.
  • Roadmap Formal penetration test by a named third-party firm. Planned before the first enterprise contract. We are not aware of any open vulnerabilities in the current production stack, but we have not commissioned an independent assessment.
  • Out of scope HIPAA and PCI DSS — out of scope. CJI processes no health data and handles no payment card data.
  • Pending DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) before any live internal customer data is processed. CJI Pulse — the product that would process internal telemetry — does not yet operate on real customer data. The DPIA is a prerequisite for that activation.

If the absence of any of these items is a blocker for your organisation, we want to know. Email hello@cjipro.com and tell us what you need. We will be direct about the timeline.
